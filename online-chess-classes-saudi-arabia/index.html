Chess in Saudi Arabia: The Vision 2030 Chess Revolution

Saudi Arabia is experiencing one of the most dramatic chess booms in the world. Under the Kingdom's Vision 2030 initiative — which aims to diversify Saudi society, economy, and cultural life — chess has been identified as a key sport for youth development and international engagement. The Saudi Chess Federation has dramatically expanded its activities over the past several years, hosting world-class international tournaments, launching school chess programmes across all regions, and registering rapidly growing numbers of FIDE-rated players.

Riyadh, Jeddah, and Dammam have all hosted major chess events in recent years, and the Kingdom's ambition to develop a generation of strong players is backed by significant institutional support. For families across Saudi Arabia — both Saudi nationals and the large expat communities from India, the UK, the US, and Southeast Asia — the timing has never been better to begin or deepen a chess education.

FIDE Rating Goals in the Saudi Competitive Scene

The Saudi Chess Federation uses the standard FIDE rating system.
